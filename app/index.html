About

Computer programing scratches both a creative and logical itch at the same time. Being able to create something new that will be useful is satisfying, but so is solving the logic puzzles themselves. I have always been a computer hobbyist and eventually couldn’t resist diving deeper into how the software was made. Before I turned to programming I gathered some valuable background. In 2012 I organized a trip to China and nearby countries. In Xian, I worked for 12 weeks with orphaned infants with special needs. I learned from putting together the complex logistics of such a trip, but also, from being immersed in such a different cultural and vocational setting. After graduating in Neuroscience at the University of Southern California in 2015, where I had the opportunity to participate in brain research, I went on for a Masters in bioimaging at Boston University. In those settings I learned to use MATLAB and R for running fMRI experiments and statistical analyses. In my scientific research I have also learned how to shift through copious documentation, and as a floor manager at a retail outlet I learned to work with and help manage a team of people.

In the summer of 2019, at Burlington Code Academy, I learned the MERN stack using mongoDB express react and node to create projects. We also learned workflow practices such as using git for version control and agile. As my capstone project, my team worked with You Need A Budget (YNAB) to bring Google Assistant functionality to their program. Now, with our extension, you can use voice activation to check your balance, add a transaction, and manage your budget. We are currently in alpha testing and will have a full release soon. In this project we used Dialogueflow and Firebase to integrate with YNAB API.

I am a member of the local group, Code for BTV, where I collaborate with other programmers on civic software and open data projects for governmental and private organizations in Vermont. For example, I am working on Money On My Mind, an app that will help people complete the Financial Futures program that helps qualifying Vermonters learn more about money management, building financial stability, and increasing household savings. Next, I will be working on an app to help Vermonters find out where to vote. In my spare time, I help to organize meetings of Burlington’s board game community. Our group now has over 90 members and counting. You can check us out on meetup.com, and maybe come and join!
